Neem (Azadirachta indica)

General Description

Neem is a fast-growing evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. It has a wide, spreading crown and long, compound leaves that are dark green in color. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers and oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds. Neem is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a vital resource in many tropical and subtropical regions.

Medicinal Uses

Neem has antibacterial, antifungal, antiviral, and anti-inflammatory properties. It treats acne, eczema, and infections, promotes oral hygiene, detoxifies the body, supports liver health, and aids wound healing. Neem oil is used as a natural insect repellent and pesticide.

Use in AYUSH Systems

  • Ayurveda: For skin disorders, detoxification, and diabetes.
  • Unani: Controls blood sugar and treats infections.
  • Siddha: Used for scalp issues and infections.
  • Homeopathy: For chronic skin and digestive issues.

Season and Growth

Evergreen tree thriving in tropical climates; flowers in spring, with seeds collected during June-August.

Where Found

Native to India, widely grown in Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, and tropical regions worldwide.
