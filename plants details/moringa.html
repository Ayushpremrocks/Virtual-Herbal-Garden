Moringa (Moringa oleifera)

General Description

Moringa, also known as the “Drumstick Tree” or “Miracle Tree,” is a fast-growing, drought-resistant tree native to the Indian subcontinent. It can grow up to 10 meters in height and is characterized by its feathery leaves and small white flowers. Almost every part of the tree is edible and has health benefits.

Medicinal Uses

Moringa is packed with nutrients and antioxidants. It reduces inflammation, regulates blood sugar, lowers cholesterol, improves digestion, and supports heart and liver health. Its leaves boost immunity and fight malnutrition. Moringa oil is used for skincare due to its moisturizing properties.

Use in AYUSH Systems

  • Ayurveda: Used as a tonic for energy, immunity, and detoxification.
  • Siddha: Recommended for joint pain, diabetes, and skin disorders.

Season and Growth

Moringa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and grows throughout the year. It is drought-resistant and grows best in well-drained soil with full sunlight.

Where Found

Native to India, widely cultivated in Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh, and parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.
