Punarnava (Boerhavia diffusa)

General Description

Punarnava is a sprawling perennial herb characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, pinkish-purple flowers. It grows close to the ground and can spread across large areas. The plant has a strong root system, which is harvested for its medicinal properties.

Medicinal Uses

Punarnava, known as the "renewing herb," is valued for its diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties. It helps in reducing edema (swelling) and promotes kidney health by flushing out toxins. Punarnava is also used to support liver function, enhance digestion, and improve respiratory health. Its roots are believed to have rejuvenating properties, making it useful in treating chronic ailments and promoting overall vitality.

Use in AYUSH Systems

  • Ayurveda: Used to balance Kapha and Pitta doshas, especially in conditions related to the kidneys and urinary tract. It is often included in formulations for detoxification and rejuvenation.
  • Unani: Prescribed for its benefits in treating liver disorders and respiratory issues.

Season and Growth

Punarnava is a perennial herb that thrives in warm climates. It grows in well-drained soils and is typically planted in the monsoon season. The plant can be harvested throughout the year, especially during the rainy season when it is most abundant.

Where Found

Native to India, Punarnava is commonly found in regions like Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, and Rajasthan. It is also found in parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.
