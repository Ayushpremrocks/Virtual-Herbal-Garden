Fennel (Foeniculum vulgare)

General Description

Fennel is a flowering plant in the carrot family, characterized by its tall stalks, feathery leaves, and yellow flowers. The plant can grow up to 2 meters in height, and its seeds have a sweet, anise-like flavor. Both the seeds and the bulbous stalks are edible and are used in cooking and herbal medicine.

Medicinal Uses

Fennel is known for its digestive benefits, reducing bloating, gas, and indigestion. It promotes appetite, relieves menstrual cramps, supports lactation, and has mild diuretic properties. Fennel seeds also help freshen breath and soothe respiratory issues like cough and asthma.

Use in AYUSH Systems

  • Ayurveda: Used in digestive tonics and to relieve colic in children.
  • Unani: Prescribed for indigestion and to increase milk production in nursing mothers.
  • Siddha: Recommended for respiratory relief and cooling the body.

Season and Growth

Fennel is a cool-season crop, planted in October-November and harvested in February-March. It grows well in well-drained soils with mild climates.

Where Found

Cultivated mainly in Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, and Gujarat. It also grows in the Mediterranean region and temperate parts of Asia and Europe.
