Karela (Momordica charantia)

General Description

Karela, commonly known as bitter gourd or bitter melon, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive bitter taste, spiky appearance, and green color. The fruit is commonly used in cooking and traditional medicine, especially in Asian cuisines.

Uses

  • Blood Sugar Regulation: Helps manage blood sugar levels, beneficial for diabetes.
  • Weight Management: Low in calories and high in fiber, aids in weight loss.
  • Digestive Health: Promotes healthy digestion and prevents constipation.
  • Immune System Support: Boosts the immune system due to vitamins and minerals.

Use in AYUSH

  • Ayurveda: Used for diabetes and digestive issues; balances Pitta and Kapha doshas.
  • Unani: Manages diabetes and improves digestive health.
  • Siddha: Important for detoxification and treating liver disorders.

Season and Growing Conditions

Karela is typically grown in warm climates as a summer crop, thriving in regions with a warm growing season and prefers well-drained, fertile soil with plenty of sunlight.

Where It Is Found

Karela is native to tropical regions and is commonly found in Asia, Africa, and the Caribbean. It is widely cultivated in countries like India, China, Indonesia, and the Philippines.
