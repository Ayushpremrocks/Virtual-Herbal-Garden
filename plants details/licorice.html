Licorice (Glycyrrhiza glabra)

General Description

Licorice is a perennial herb with a deep, fleshy root and upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. It features compound leaves and produces attractive purple flowers. The root has a sweet flavor and is commonly used in herbal remedies and culinary applications.

Medicinal Uses

Licorice root is known for its soothing properties and is commonly used to treat digestive issues, such as heartburn and ulcers. It has anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, and antioxidant effects, making it beneficial for respiratory conditions like coughs and bronchitis. Licorice also helps soothe sore throats and may support adrenal health.

Use in AYUSH Systems

  • Ayurveda: Used as a demulcent to soothe the gastrointestinal tract and alleviate respiratory issues.
  • Unani: Applied for its soothing properties in treating coughs and infections.
  • Siddha: Used in formulations to relieve cough and enhance overall vitality.

Season and Growth

Licorice is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils. It is typically planted in spring and harvested after 3-4 years in late summer or early autumn.

Where Found

Native to parts of Southern Europe and Asia, licorice is cultivated in regions such as Turkey, Iran, and India (particularly in the Himalayan region).
