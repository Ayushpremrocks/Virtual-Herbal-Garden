Holy Basil (Tulsi) (Ocimum sanctum / Ocimum tenuiflorum)

General Description

Tulsi is a sacred herb in Hindu culture, often referred to as the "Queen of Herbs." It is a small shrub with green to purple leaves and can grow up to 3 feet tall. Tulsi produces small, aromatic white or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is cherished not only for its medicinal properties but also for its spiritual significance, often found in temples and homes.

Medicinal Uses

Tulsi is highly valued for its immune-boosting, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. It helps treat colds, coughs, and respiratory issues. It is also known to reduce stress, regulate blood sugar levels, and support cardiovascular health.

Use in AYUSH Systems

  • Ayurveda: Considered a sacred plant, Tulsi is used to treat respiratory disorders, stress, and infections.
  • Unani: Used for fever, bronchitis, and skin disorders.
  • Siddha: Recommended for respiratory and digestive problems.
  • Homeopathy: Administered for chronic cough and sinus issues.

Season and Growth

Tulsi grows throughout the year and is tolerant of various climatic conditions. It thrives in warm, tropical environments and is commonly grown in gardens and temple courtyards.

Where Found

Tulsi is cultivated across India and Southeast Asia. It is frequently found in households due to its religious and medicinal significance.
