Cardamom (Elettaria cardamomum)

General Description

Known as the "Queen of Spices," cardamom is a highly valued spice with a distinctive aroma. Belonging to the ginger family, it is widely used in cooking, traditional medicine, and perfumes.

Types:

  • Green Cardamom: Used in both sweet and savory dishes, and traditional medicines.
  • Black Cardamom: Smokier flavor, mainly used in savory dishes.

Uses

  • Digestive Health: Improves digestion, reduces bloating, and relieves nausea.
  • Oral Health: Freshens breath and helps maintain oral hygiene.
  • Respiratory Health: Relieves cough, colds, and clears congestion.
  • Antioxidant & Anti-inflammatory: Fights oxidative stress and reduces inflammation.
  • Blood Pressure Regulation: May lower blood pressure with diuretic effects.
  • Detoxification: Helps cleanse kidneys and reduce UTIs.
  • Mental Health: Used in aromatherapy to relieve stress and anxiety.
  • Weight Loss: Boosts metabolism and promotes fat burning.

Use in AYUSH

  • Ayurveda: Balances Vata and Kapha doshas, aids digestion, and supports respiratory health.
  • Unani: Used as a warming herb to treat digestive, respiratory, and heart-related conditions.
  • Siddha: Helps with digestion and respiratory issues, balancing body heat and phlegm.

Season and Growing Conditions

Cardamom flowers during the rainy season (June to November), with harvesting taking place between October and December.

It thrives in tropical rainforests with rich, moist soil, a warm climate, and plenty of shade.

Geographical Conditions

Native to the forests of southern India and Sri Lanka, cardamom is now grown in tropical regions worldwide. The Western Ghats of India—particularly Kerala, Karnataka, and Tamil Nadu—are major producers, along with countries like Guatemala and Tanzania.
