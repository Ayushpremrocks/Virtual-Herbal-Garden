Aloe Vera (Aloe barbadensis miller)

Medicinal Uses

Aloe Vera is well-known for its soothing, anti-inflammatory, and moisturizing properties. The gel extracted from its leaves is used to treat burns, cuts, and skin irritations. It also supports digestion, helps relieve constipation, and promotes wound healing. Aloe Vera has detoxifying properties and can enhance liver function.

Use in AYUSH Systems

  • Ayurveda: Aloe is used as a Rasayana (rejuvenative) and for treating skin disorders, digestive issues, and menstrual problems.
  • Unani: Considered a cooling agent, it is used for liver-related problems and skin ailments.
  • Siddha: Aloe is applied externally to reduce heat and inflammation.
  • Homeopathy: Used for digestive complaints, especially diarrhea and colitis.

Season and Growth

Aloe Vera is a hardy plant that thrives throughout the year, especially in hot and arid climates. It grows well in tropical and subtropical areas and is resistant to drought. The plant prefers well-drained soil and plenty of sunlight. It is mostly harvested during the summer months when the gel content inside the leaves is at its peak.

Where Found

Aloe Vera is cultivated extensively in Rajasthan, Gujarat, Maharashtra, and southern Indian states. Globally, it is grown in arid and semi-arid regions like Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, and the Americas.
