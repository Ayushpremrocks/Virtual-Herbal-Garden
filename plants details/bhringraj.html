Bhringraj (Eclipta prostrata)

General Description

Bhringraj, the "King of Herbs for Hair," is a small herb used in Ayurveda to promote hair growth and enhance liver function. It thrives in moist areas and is known for its therapeutic properties, particularly for hair and scalp care.

Uses

  • Hair Growth: Stimulates hair growth and reduces hair loss.
  • Liver Health: Detoxifies and protects the liver, aids in cell regeneration.
  • Skin Health: Anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial, effective for acne and wounds.
  • Cognitive Function: Enhances memory, calms the mind, reduces stress.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Reduces pain and inflammation, especially for arthritis.
  • Blood Purification: Purifies blood and treats skin conditions.
  • Respiratory Health: Helps in asthma and cough due to expectorant properties.

Use in AYUSH

  • Ayurveda: Used for hair, liver, and respiratory issues, known for rejuvenating properties.
  • Siddha: Treats jaundice and skin diseases, beneficial for hair and liver health.

Season and Growing Conditions

Bhringraj thrives in warm, humid conditions during the monsoon and prefers moist, fertile soil.

Where It Is Found

Native to India, China, Thailand, and Brazil, Bhringraj is widely found in humid climates, especially in Kerala, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, and Maharashtra.
