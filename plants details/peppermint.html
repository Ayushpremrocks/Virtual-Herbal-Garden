General Description

Peppermint is a hybrid mint plant, a cross between watermint and spearmint. It is known for its cooling aroma and medicinal properties.

Real-Life Uses

  • Digestive Aid: Relieves indigestion and IBS.
  • Respiratory Health: Menthol eases coughs and colds.
  • Pain Relief: Applied topically for headaches and muscle pain.
  • Oral Health: Used in toothpaste for fresh breath.
  • Stress Relief: Popular in aromatherapy.

Growing Conditions

Peppermint thrives in moist, well-drained soil with partial shade. It grows best during summer months from May to August.

Geographical Conditions

Peppermint is native to Europe and the Middle East but is now cultivated worldwide, especially in temperate regions. Major producers include the United States, India, China, and parts of Europe.
