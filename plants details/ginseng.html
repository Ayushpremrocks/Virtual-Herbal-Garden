Ginseng (Panax ginseng)

General Description

Ginseng is a perennial plant characterized by its fleshy roots and umbrella-like leaves. It typically grows to about 30 cm in height and has small, greenish-white flowers. Known for its adaptogenic properties, Ginseng has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Medicinal Uses

Ginseng is renowned as an adaptogen, helping the body manage stress and boost energy levels. It enhances cognitive function, improves mood, reduces fatigue, and supports the immune system. Ginseng also promotes heart health, regulates blood sugar, and may have anti-cancer properties.

Use in AYUSH Systems

  • Unani: Used to enhance endurance, reduce stress, and boost immunity.
  • Ayurveda: Occasionally included for vitality and energy-boosting formulations.

Season and Growth

Ginseng grows best in cool climates with well-drained, shaded areas. It requires 4-6 years to mature and is harvested between September and October.

Where Found

Native to Korea, China, and parts of Siberia, with some cultivation in the United States and Canada. Panax ginseng (Asian ginseng) is the most potent, while American ginseng (Panax quinquefolius) offers similar benefits with milder effects.
